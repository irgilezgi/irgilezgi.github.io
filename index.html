§1 About

I am an Assistant Professor (Profesor Ayudante Doctor) in the Department of Social Sciences at Universidad Carlos III de Madrid (UC3M). My work lies at the intersection of politics and forced migration, particularly focusing on migration diplomacy and refugee commodification through international relations, everyday politics of forced migration with a focus on political behaviour and attitudes, and anti-immigrant political parties in the Global South.

Before joining UC3M, I was a Postdoctoral Research Fellow and later an Associate Researcher in the Global Politics and Security Programme at the Swedish Institute of International Affairs (UI). I hold a Ph.D. in Political Science from the University of Gothenburg, where my dissertation, Everyday Politics of Forced Migration, is available open access. I also hold an M.A. in International Affairs from the Elliott School of International Affairs, George Washington University, and a B.A. in Political Science and International Relations from Boğaziçi University.
